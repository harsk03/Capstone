A.
Introduction

Remote Photoplethysmography (rPPG) recovers heart rate from subtle periodic color changes in facial skin caused by pulsatile blood flow, captured by a standard webcam — no contact required.

Existing systems implement only a single estimation method and provide raw BPM with no clinical context. This project addresses eight literature-identified gaps by implementing four signal processing methods, adding HRV analysis, stress detection, and automated PDF clinical reporting — all on consumer hardware, no GPU needed.

B.
Research Gaps Addressed
  • Lighting sensitivity — Lighting Quality Assessor (CLAHE + LAB channel)
  • Motion artifacts — Optical flow motion gate + landmark stability check
  • Single method — FFT, CHROM, ICA, POS fused via SNR weighting
  • No clinical output — HRV metrics, stress detection, PDF report
  • GPU dependency — CPU-only pipeline at 30 fps on i5 laptop
  • Single ROI — Forehead + Bilateral Cheeks via MediaPipe FaceMesh

Clinical Significance & Future Scope
A Webcam as Your Cardiologist! ❤️

Our system turns any standard laptop camera into a real-time, non-contact heart monitor with clinically relevant accuracy — no wearable, no electrode, no cost. It runs entirely in Python on a basic laptop, making it accessible for home health monitoring and telemedicine.

E.
Methodology & Algorithms
1) FFT – Green Channel
  • Green channel has strongest plethysmographic signal.
  • Band-pass filter: 0.67–3.33 Hz (40–200 BPM).
  • Dominant frequency in FFT gives BPM. SNR-weighted; scaled ×0.25 in fusion.
  • Role: Fast, stable baseline.
2) CHROM Algorithm
  • Xs = 3Rn − 2Gn, Ys = 1.5Rn + Gn − 1.5Bn.
  • Adaptive α = σ(Xs)/σ(Ys); S = Xs − αYs.
  • Cancels specular reflection & illumination changes.
  • Role: Best in variable lighting.
3) POS Algorithm
  • H1 = G−B, H2 = G+B−2R; β = σ(H1)/σ(H2).
  • S = H1 + β·H2 (skin-tone orthogonal projection).
  • Robust to slow illumination drift; computationally lightest.
  • Role: Best in motion-stable scenes.
4) ICA – Blind Source Separation
  • Fixed-point FastICA via RGB covariance eigendecomposition.
  • Component selected by max pulse-band power.
  • Conditionally run when methods disagree (>12 BPM).
  • Role: Signal recovery in low-SNR.
5) HRV & Stress Metrics
  • SDNN / RMSSD / pNN50 from RR intervals (60s window).
  • Stress Score: 100 − clamp(RMSSD/50 × 100); 4 levels.
  • Alert System: 7-level BPM classification.
  • Role: Clinical-grade wellness insight.
6) SNR-Weighted Fusion
  • Weight per method: w = 10^(SNR/10); FFT scaled ×0.25.
  • Agreement bonus ×2 for methods within 8 BPM of median.
  • Final BPM weighted average + Kalman filter (R=2.0).
  • Role: Maximizes accuracy and stability.

Key Outcomes

  • SNR fusion outperforms every individual method.
  • CHROM achieves best single-method accuracy (MAE 5.43 BPM).
  • MediaPipe ROI vastly improves accuracy over Haar.
  • Kalman filter reduces BPM output variance.
  • Age/Gender accuracy: 74% / 89%.
  • Full 42-subject pipeline: ~18 min on i5 CPU.
  • PDF reports generated in <2 seconds.
